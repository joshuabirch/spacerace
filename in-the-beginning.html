A Brief Timeline...

Between the years 1955 and 1975, the world saw the quickest innovation of aeronuatical engineering, technology and political sovereignty of all time, take a look at how it unfolded through the timeline below!

  • Second World War Ends 1945

    The US and USSR ally each other to seek victory in the war against Nazi Germany despite never being historically friendly. With Soviet Leader Stalin looking to consolidate his control in european countries, the ever looking United States grow more worried.

  • The Truman Proposition 1947

    President Truman vows to help nations that are threatened by the soviet's attempts to expand. Truman's strategy was to offer financial and military support to any anti-communist forces. This intention of opposition leads to the Cold War which is named after the lack of 'heat'. Neither side truly fought against each other.

  • Substitution War 1946 - 1955

    Instead of direct conflict, the two nations clash politically by supporting regional wars in the Middle East, Africa, South America, Korea, China, Cuba and Veitnam.

  • United States Plans for Satellite 1955

    The Space Race officially begins as the United States announces that it intends to launch the first satellite to outer space. The Soviets respond by saying it will also launch a satellite "in the near future".

  • Sputnik Launches 1957

    The Soviets launch Sputnik 1 into space, an artificial satellite which orbited for 3 weeks before its batteries died.

  • Space Race Merges into US Elections 1960

    With the Americans angered that the Russians "did it first", John F Kennedy began a political platform of closing the gap in the Space Race.

  • Yuri Gagarin Launches into Space 1961

    A year later, the Russians beat the Americans again by sending the first man into space. He completed an orbit of Earth in Vostock 1 becoming a soviet hero. Kennedy previously said that the US space program "too expensive" but now says he intends to spend whatever to catch up.

  • John Glenn Orbits Earth 1962

    America send their man John Glenn into orbit a year later thanks to NASA's team of 'human computers' who made complex calculations by hand, those of which were need for the trajectories of the flight in order to succeed.

  • The Race to the Moon 1963 - 1969

    The Americans begin launching the Apollo missions which the Russians begin to develop their Voskhod and Soyuz crafts. Both sides suffer setbacks as the race to the moon continues to heat up.

  • Apollo 11 Moon Landing July 20th 1969

    The Saturn V rocket successful takes Neil Armstrong, Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin Jr and Michael Collins to the Moon and clinches 'victory' for the United States. Watched by around 723 million people, humanity witnessed man on Earth's natural satellite.

  • The Space Race Slows Down 1970

    With the lunar goal achieved, both sides began to slow down its efforts with space missions. NASA's plans weren't well received by the american government as well as the USSR failing to reach the moon which was eventually cancelled.
